## Successes & Strengths

1. **Carryover discipline matured into deliberate pre-epic preparation.** Commit `32f6078` (pre-Epic-6 cleanup) closed three live-trading exit gates BEFORE Story 6.1 started: D-1 (panic mode wired into `permits_trading()` + `DenialReason::PanicModeActive`); D-2 producer side (per-tick anomaly producer + `FlattenRequest` channel; consumer-side drain remains Story 8.2's); D-3 (fee-staleness deduplicated to one canonical owner — `CircuitBreakers::check_fee_staleness`; `FeeGate` reduced to pure economics). Epic 4 introduced carryover; Epic 5 institutionalized it inside the next story; Epic 6 elevated it to a focused cleanup commit when the next story couldn't naturally absorb it.

2. **Structural invariants over docstring contracts.** The 6.2 orchestrator type has zero fields, zero parameters, and zero method signatures referencing order or position management. Test 7.9 (`disabling_strategy_has_no_access_to_order_state`) makes the never-cancel-stops invariant a *compile-time* guarantee — a future regression that adds an order-management reference would fail to compile. This is the direct answer to Epic 5's "docstring-as-contract was the load-bearing safety mechanism in three places" challenge.

3. **Spec-vs-AC tension caught at implementation, resolved on the safety side.** Story 6.2 Task 4.1 said "if `conservative_on_oscillation`, do NOT update current_regime — remain in current (more conservative) state." The AC said "system remains in the more conservative state." These conflict when the proposed regime is *safer* than current (e.g., Trending currently active, Volatile proposed during cooldown — literal spec leaves system in Trending, which is *less* conservative). Dev recognized the contradiction, implemented "collapse to `max(conservative_rank(current), proposed))`" so oscillation can only ever degrade to a safer state, and documented the deviation explicitly. Test 7.11 verifies both sides (Trending→Volatile collapses to Volatile; subsequent Volatile→Rotational does NOT promote back). Reviewer accepted as defensible safety improvement.

4. **Hot-path discipline reflexive.** Single allocation at construction (6.1's ring buffer; 6.2's HashMap/Vec); `try_send` non-blocking journal forwarding (6.2 follows the canonical pattern from 5.1 / 5.6); `#![deny(unsafe_code)]` propagated through both new modules; clippy clean; new files zero fmt drift. No regression.

5. **Test density.** ~34 new tests with explicit boundary cases: warmup transition (6.1), conservative ordering (6.2 `conservative_ordering_is_correct` exercises all four-way combinations), cooldown=0 short-circuit (6.2), TOML round-trip (6.1 + 6.2), ring-buffer bounded growth over 1k updates (6.1 invariant test), `current()` stickiness between updates (6.1 invariant test).

6. **Story 6.1 untouched by 6.2.** `crates/engine/src/regime/threshold.rs` was not modified during 6.2 work. The only `mod.rs` change was adding `pub mod orchestrator;`. All 11 of Story 6.1's tests still pass post-6.2. Clean separation between detector (signal-domain) and orchestrator (orchestration-domain) preserved.

## Challenges & Growth Areas

### Decision-needed → auto-defer pattern shipped two real design questions as field comments

The 6.2 review explicitly auto-converted two `decision-needed` items into deferrals "per non-interactive policy":

- **S-1 (cooldown anchor on conservative collapse).** When `conservative_on_oscillation = true` and an oscillation collapses `current_regime` to a safer state (orchestrator DOES update `current_regime` and `enabled_strategies`), `last_transition_time` is NOT updated. Subsequent cooldown checks anchor to the original transition time, not to the collapse instant. The field doc comment ("last timestamp at which a transition was acted upon") is inconsistent with the behavior — a conservative collapse IS an acted-upon state change. Reviewer auto-deferred to "document the single-anchor semantics in the field comment."

- **S-2 (`pending_transitions` Vec grows unbounded).** Cooldown-suppressed oscillations push to a `Vec<RegimeTransition>` that is never drained or cleared. Only consumer is a test helper. In a long-running session with persistent oscillation near a threshold boundary, the vector grows monotonically (~1 entry/bar during oscillation). Reviewer auto-deferred to "clear on cooldown expiry."

Both deferrals are defensible — neither is a live-trading risk in the current usage pattern. The systemic concern: real design ambiguities ("does the cooldown anchor reset on collapse?", "should pending_transitions exist at all?") got resolved by convention rather than explicit decision. This is a milder rerun of Epic 5's docstring-as-contract finding: load-bearing semantics living in a comment instead of in the type or in tests.

**Resolution this retro:** S-2 is upgraded to an Epic 7 prep item because of Epic 7's determinism criteria — see Action Item A-1 below. S-1 stays deferred but the field comment must be added before Epic 7 starts. See Action Item A-2.

### P-4 AC semantic-precision pass did not fire at story creation

Epic 5 retro's P-4 commitment: *"Threshold tasks must explicitly state comparison semantics... Architect reviews AC before dev starts."* Story 6.2's spec-vs-AC tension on conservative-on-oscillation was visible in the task spec text — the conflict between "do NOT update current_regime" (mechanism) and "system remains in the more conservative state" (outcome) is a P-4 candidate. The dev caught it at implementation and resolved it correctly, but P-4 was meant to catch it earlier.

This is mild — outcome was correct, no rework, no SHOULD-FIX. But Epic 7 has explicit determinism ACs ("RegimeState values are identical at every bar boundary"; "FixedPrice values are bit-identical") where AC ambiguity has bigger blast radius. P-4 needs reinforcement before Epic 7 story creation.

**Resolution:** Action item P-6 below — extend P-4 to determinism semantics specifically.

### Story 6.1's NICE-TO-HAVE findings did not get carryover-absorbed

Epic 5 celebrated 5.2's organic absorption of 5.1's S-1 finding (the four entry points updated `DenialReason` numeric-context fields BEFORE the trip check, dissolving the gap). Story 6.2 didn't touch `threshold.rs` and so absorbed none of 6.1's four NICE-TO-HAVE findings (missing classify-quadrant test, `r1 = high - low` without `.abs()`, redundant capacity guard, dead `atr_trending_threshold` field).

This is not a regression. 6.1 had no SHOULD-FIX to absorb — only NICE-TO-HAVE. The carryover-fix muscle didn't get a workout because nothing was load-bearing enough to require absorption. Worth flagging that the discipline only fires when there's something genuinely worth fixing, not as a checkbox.

## Action Items

### Process Improvements

**P-6 (extends P-4). AC semantic-precision pass for determinism criteria.**
At Story 7.1 / 7.2 creation, Architect reviews ACs for semantic ambiguity around determinism: "identical" must specify scope (state? observable behavior? internal counters?); "bit-identical" applies only to types with deterministic-encoded representations (FixedPrice, integers — not f64); "ε-tolerant" must specify the epsilon explicitly. P-4 was loose on mechanism-vs-outcome (6.2 caught it at impl); Epic 7 has higher blast radius and needs the pass to fire earlier.
**Why:** 6.2's spec-vs-AC tension on conservative-on-oscillation was caught at impl time. Epic 7's determinism criteria are exactly where ambiguity compounds — a vague "identical RegimeState" AC could pass tests under one reasonable interpretation and fail under another.
**Owner:** Winston (Architect) reviews AC; story creator (Amelia / bmad-create-story) drafts.
**Success:** Epic 7 stories have explicit determinism semantics on every AC; no semantic-drift findings at code review.
**Deadline:** Apply when creating Epic 7 stories.

**P-7. Decision-needed handling discipline.**
When a code reviewer raises a `decision-needed` item that is auto-converted to defer "per non-interactive policy," the deferral must include a short rationale (one sentence: why this default is safe in current usage) AND a forward reference (which future story would naturally resolve it). The 6.2 S-1 and S-2 deferrals already carry this; codify it as the norm so future auto-deferrals don't lose context.
**Why:** Two real design questions in 6.2 got resolved by convention. Documenting why-it's-safe-now and where-it-resolves makes the deferral auditable.
**Owner:** Reviewer (whoever runs `bmad-code-review`).
**Success:** All future auto-deferrals in code reviews include both a rationale and a forward owner.
**Deadline:** In effect immediately.
